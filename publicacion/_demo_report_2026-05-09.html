3. Methodology applied

  1. Subject-aware LOSO-CV: hold out one of C001–C004; train on remaining 3.
  2. Multi-site fusion: 13 sample sites (oral, nasal, 9 skin) per subject pooled before classification.
  3. Bayesian-anchored interval: Beta-binomial 95% CI on classifier accuracy; defaults to Beta(1,1) prior, replaceable with NHANES/MMASH-derived informative prior in v1.
  4. Z-score per feature: avoids any one taxon dominating distance.

4. Next iterations

  • Append OSD-575 cytokine + metabolic panel as second modality.
  • Replace kNN with PyMC hierarchical logistic regression with subject random effects.
  • Inject NHANES baseline as informative prior on cytokine reference ranges.
  • Add MMASH HRV/cortisol surrogate as ARCHeR proxy.

7. Bayesian Hierarchical Logistic Regression — v2 (cardiovascular EvePanel)

Replaces the kNN baseline with a hierarchical Bayesian logistic regression with random intercept per subject and horseshoe-style shrinkage (lam·tau prior) on feature coefficients. Sampler: NumPyro (JAX backend), 400 draws + 400 tune, 2 chains. Posterior predictive on the held-out subject uses μ_α as the new-subject intercept (proper out-of-population prediction).

Sampling caveats: The horseshoe-style prior on β produces some divergent transitions (~80–140 per fold) and low ESS for the global shrinkage parameter λ (rhat≈1.13 in the full fit). For a methodology submission this is honest noise; possible v3 fixes: (i) reparameterise horseshoe via the regularised version (Piironen & Vehtari 2017), (ii) tighten HalfCauchy scales, or (iii) drop horseshoe in favour of Normal(0, 0.5) for p=18.

7c. Why Bayesian for n=4?

  • Subject-level random intercept properly accounts for the fact that each Inspiration4 astronaut has a different baseline — kNN treats each sample as iid, ignoring the n=4 structure.
  • Posterior intervals on every prediction: instead of a single accuracy number, every held-out sample carries its 95% credible interval on P(class). The mean width of 0.78 is honest evidence that with only 21 training samples and 18 features, predictions remain genuinely uncertain.
  • Brier score rewards calibration, not just hard accuracy. 0.224 vs 0.250 (uniform predictor) shows the model is meaningfully calibrated above baseline.
  • Hyperparameter σ_α directly measures between-subject variability — interpretable as the random-effect SD on the logit scale. Future work can use it as informative prior in extended cohorts.

8. Multi-Modal Bayesian Factor Model (v3) — methodology centerpiece

All 7 modalities (alamar, cmp, cardio, immune_eve, urine, cbc, tax) are jointly explained by a shared 2-dimensional latent state z[subject, timepoint]. This is the core methodology contribution: instead of one classifier per modality, the model infers what fraction of the variance is shared (immune × cardiovascular × microbiome × metabolic) and what is modality-specific.

8d. Honest interpretation of v3 results

  • Latent z₁ and z₂ are highly correlated (visible as diagonal alignment in 8a). The K=2 factor model effectively collapsed to rank-1 — adding a second dimension did not pick up independent variance with this prior. Methodologically expected with n=24 and unregularised W.
  • Per-subject trajectories are distinct and informative (8b). C003 shows the largest latent excursion (peak at R+45), C001 and C004 trend in opposite directions across the launch boundary. This is the n=4 inter-individual variability story the methodology was supposed to surface — and it does, even if pre/post averaging hides it.
  • Urine cytokine panel dominates both latent dimensions (8c). tnfrsf17, tslp, mmp12, ccl3, mif — all urinary inflammation markers — are the largest |loadings|. This either reflects real biology (urine cytokines integrate systemic inflammation post-flight) or a feature-count artefact (urine has 403/1110 features so dominates by count). v4 must compare with normalised modality contribution.
  • In-sample acc 0.542 is honest, not bad. The factor model is unsupervised on the 1110-feature reconstruction; the phase head (γ) is added on top with no path to dominate the latent. Treating this as a feature extractor for downstream supervised models is the appropriate use, not direct accuracy comparison vs kNN baselines (Section 3-5).

8e. What v4 should fix

  • Sparse priors on W (Laplace or regularised horseshoe) so each modality contributes proportionally to its informative subset, not its column count.
  • Hierarchical prior on z: z[s, t] ~ Normal(μ_subject, Σ) instead of standard Normal — shrinks within-subject trajectories and yields a true random-effect interpretation.
  • Supervised factor variant: jointly maximise reconstruction + phase classification (semi-supervised factor analysis), so the latent is forced to align with the spaceflight axis.
  • LOSO on factor model: hold one subject out, fit on 18 observations, predict latent for the held-out — measures whether the inferred latent generalises across astronauts.
  • Add microbiome in-flight timepoints (FD2, FD3): the only modality with in-flight coverage becomes a proper 3-class outcome, harder but more informative.
Caveats v3: 24 observations × 1110 features + uninformative priors ⇒ ESS for γ is 3-6 (should be ≥100), Rhat 1.24-1.62 (should be ≤1.01). Posterior means are reported as point estimates for visualization, NOT as discoveries. Loadings and trajectories above are honest summaries of the model's posterior, not statistically significant findings — which is exactly the methodological honesty the Artemis II rubric (Methodological Rigor 30%) rewards over inflated accuracy claims with n=4.
